The list was not dramatic. It included apologies, explanations, small admirations, corrections, and one ordinary thank-you that had somehow become impossible through delay. The numbering gave each sentence a false sense of discipline, as if restraint could be audited into virtue.

Six items repeated under different dates. One line had been written in pencil, erased, and written again with less confidence. Nothing on the list was rare. That was the accusation.

A life can become crowded with words that should have been cheap to spend.

## Case Notes

The inventory is not a diary; it is closer to a warehouse ledger. Its categories are emotional but its method is bureaucratic, which makes the withheld sentences feel less private and more like goods trapped in storage.

The unresolved question is whether counting the unsaid prepares it for release, or simply gives silence a better filing system.
