Each month had a title before it had events. June was for apprenticeship. September was for a city by water. December was for telling the truth with fewer witnesses. The calendar did not plan appointments; it planned temperament.

The alternate name appeared first as a signature exercise, then as a label for a life with different rooms, different clothes, and a smaller tolerance for apology. It was not an alias. It was a weather system for a self that had not formed.

The calendar did not fail. It stayed soft, which is what some futures do when they are never asked to become dates.

## Name Weather

The artifact is filed under Unlived because it does not describe a fantasy. It describes an administrative possibility: the person who might have emerged if paperwork, place, and habit had aligned differently.

A smaller, meaner question remains: whether identity is chosen once, or rehearsed privately until one rehearsal becomes public.
