The text was short enough to be brave and late enough to be suspicious. It said almost nothing new, only that the thought had survived longer than expected.

The signal changed. The message vanished. The street accepted the decision without looking impressed.

## Draft Bubble

The text was composed under a red signal, which gave it exactly twenty-six seconds of borrowed courage. It began too formally, then too casually, then finally with the person's name and nothing after it.

The light changed. That is almost stupid as an explanation, but it is also true. The body moved before the message did, and once across the street, the sentence had lost its weather.

The draft stayed in the phone long enough to become less a message than a small fossil of timing.
