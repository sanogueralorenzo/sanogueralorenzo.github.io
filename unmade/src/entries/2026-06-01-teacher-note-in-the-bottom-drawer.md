The note avoided grand language. It said only that the work had been seen, that the quiet parts were not empty, and that the student should not mistake speed for intelligence.

It stayed in the drawer because the term ended badly. Years later, the paper still understood something the classroom had failed to say.

## Bottom Drawer

The note was written after the room emptied. That matters. Praise given in public can become another test. This one was meant to arrive without witnesses, without the student having to perform gratitude or disbelief.

The teacher rewrote the first sentence twice. Too warm. Too official. Too late. The final version was almost plain enough to survive: I saw what you were doing, including the parts you hid inside quiet work.

It stayed in the drawer because adults are also cowards in small ways. The paper kept the courage; the person did not.
