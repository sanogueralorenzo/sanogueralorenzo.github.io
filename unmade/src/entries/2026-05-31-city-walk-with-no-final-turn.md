The walk was meant to become a map for insomnia: start at the laundromat, pass the pharmacy with the flickering sign, turn where the sidewalk smells like rain even in dry weather.

It stopped before the final turn because completion would have made it usable, and usability was never the point.

## Route Card

The walk was planned without a destination because destinations make a city too obedient. The card listed turns by texture instead: left at the glossy lobby, straight past the warm bakery vent, stop where the sidewalk changes color.

It never happened. Rain is the official reason. The better reason is that open walks require a kind of permission most days do not grant. Errands are easier. Errands know when they are finished.

The missing final turn is not a flaw in the map. It is the whole map admitting that arrival was never the point.
