The memo used neutral language because neutral language is how buildings hide fear. It referred to the floor as a legacy access condition and advised staff not to discuss vertical discrepancies.

It was never sent. The lock stayed, the button stayed blank, and the elevator continued stopping there when nobody was inside.

## Distribution List

The memo was addressed to six people and clearly meant for more. Its subject line tried to sound boring. Access clarification. Temporary restriction. Operational continuity. The usual costume.

Inside, the language kept slipping. A paragraph about elevator permissions became a paragraph about who had been seen waiting outside the wrong door. A note about storage became a note about what could not be stored anywhere else.

The memo was never sent because it solved nothing. It only proved that the locked floor had become an office-wide method of not saying what everyone knew.
