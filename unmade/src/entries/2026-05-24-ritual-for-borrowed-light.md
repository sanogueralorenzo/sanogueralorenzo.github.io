The ritual was practical, almost embarrassing: sit across from someone steady, name the thing you cannot currently believe, and let them hold belief on your behalf for one week. The candle was not symbolic. It was a timer, a witness, and an excuse not to look directly at each other.

The instructions insisted on modesty. No promises. No advice unless asked for twice. No correcting the scale of another person's fear. At the end of the week, the borrowed hope could be returned, renewed, or quietly kept.

It was never formalized because the useful version already happens quietly between people who know when not to call it magic.

## Borrowed Flame

The artifact is classed as Unbuilt because it is less an event than a social technology: a repeatable structure for carrying what someone else cannot carry alone.

What still bothers the record is this: whether naming such care makes it stronger, or makes it too self-conscious to survive.
