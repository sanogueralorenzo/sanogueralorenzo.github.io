The transcript preserves everything around the absence: breath before the answer, one syllable, the scrape of a phone against a table, then the flat authority of signal loss. The missing sentence is bracketed with too much care, as if typography could recover signal.

The first reconstruction was confident. The second admitted uncertainty. The third kept only the interruption and the exact time it arrived, which may be all the call truly left behind.

What was going to be said is now impossible to separate from the failure that protected it.

## Dead Air

The transcript exposes how quickly memory becomes editorial. Every attempt to restore the dropped words made them more authored, less trustworthy, and more desired.

The last page leaves this open: whether the failed connection prevented a truth, or saved it from being reduced to a sentence.
