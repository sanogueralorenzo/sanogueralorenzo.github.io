There was a city on the other end of the ticket, and inside that city a whole sequence of rooms, errands, jobs, friends, and habits that would have felt inevitable after a month. The second ticket was bought after the first decision failed, which made it less like transport and more like an appeal.

The train left once on the schedule and once in imagination. The first departure took other passengers. The second kept returning for years, always at the same platform, always with enough time to board if the body could be convinced.

The ticket expired into evidence. Not regret exactly, but proof that a decision can remain visible long after the path closes.

## Case Notes

The artifact matters because it was not destroyed. Most unused tickets disappear into bins, drawers, or laundry. This one stayed close enough to become a portable border crossing.

Its unresolved force comes from the second departure: the one that did not happen but continued organizing the meaning of staying.
