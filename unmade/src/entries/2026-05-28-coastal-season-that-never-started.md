The season had a small apartment, salt on the windows, a grocery walk past fishing gear, and mornings measured by gull noise instead of alarms.

It never started. Still, for months afterward, every inland day felt like it had been chosen against the sea.

## Rental Notes

The saved listing mentioned bad insulation, coin laundry, and a view that was mostly parking lot if you stood too close to the window. Still, the place had the right kind of imperfection. It looked temporary enough to forgive, which is sometimes what a season needs.

A packing list was started and abandoned after six lines: sweater, notebook, cheap knife, charger, rain shoes, the blue mug. The list gives the whole plan away. This was not a move. It was a test of whether another climate could interrupt a pattern before the pattern became biography.

What never arrived was not the coast. It was the version of attention that the coast had been asked to provide.
