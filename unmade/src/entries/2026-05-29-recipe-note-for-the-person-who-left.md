The note explained where the recipe always lied: more salt than written, less heat than pride suggests, stop stirring when the smell changes from sharp to round.

It was never sent because the dish had become a border. To explain it would have been to admit the kitchen still expected company.

## Margin Corrections

The recipe's official measurements are mostly decorative. The real instructions live in the margins: wait until the onion gives up, ignore the timer, add the lemon after the heat is off. That is the part a printed card cannot inherit.

There is one crossed-out line that looks like an apology and one replacement that looks like cooking advice. The distinction may not matter. Family recipes often smuggle feeling through verbs because verbs are safer than declarations.

The note remained unsent because sending it would have admitted that taste remembers what conversation does not.
