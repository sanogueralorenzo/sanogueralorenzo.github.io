The old bridge had no drama left in it, only steel, traffic, and the quiet authority of a sign changing languages halfway across.

The crossing was postponed until postponement became the choice. On some maps, hesitation is the thickest line.

## Toll Receipt

The receipt was kept because it looked official enough to blame. It had the date, the lane number, the weather printed too faintly to read, and a charge small enough to make the hesitation embarrassing. People cross borders for less. People also refuse them for reasons they cannot make sound serious.

The bridge itself offered no drama. That may have been the problem. No guard leaning into the window, no cinematic question, no storm. Just the ordinary fact of being allowed through.

The part that stays sharp is not the country on the other side. It is the minute before the turn, when the road was still neutral and the body had not yet voted.
