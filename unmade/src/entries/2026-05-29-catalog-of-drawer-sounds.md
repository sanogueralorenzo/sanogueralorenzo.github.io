The catalog began with kitchen drawers because they were honest: knives sounded decisive, foil sounded nervous, batteries sounded like a small argument.

It stopped at the bedroom cabinet, where the sounds became too private. Not every archive should learn to classify intimacy.

## Sorting Problem

The catalog worked until it became too accurate. Kitchen drawers were easy: utensils, batteries, tape, the dry scrape of instruction manuals for appliances already gone. The sounds had categories because the rooms had jobs.

The bedroom drawer ruined the system. Coins against a receipt. A photograph sliding under a comb. The dull plastic knock of something kept for no reason except that throwing it away would have made the day too definite.

The catalog stops there, not out of laziness, but because some sounds are not household data. They are a warning that the house has been listening back.
