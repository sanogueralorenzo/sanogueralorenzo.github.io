The atlas ignored walls when walls did not matter. It measured the distance between the table and the confession, the bed and the fever, the door and the moment nobody followed.

It stopped at the legend. The symbols were clear, but too private to teach.

## Legend Draft

The first legend used normal symbols: door, window, table, bed. Those were crossed out almost immediately. The useful symbols were stranger. A small black square meant a place where someone decided not to answer. A dotted line meant a route taken only during arguments. A pale circle marked where a room looked larger at night than it did in the morning.

The atlas was never really interested in architecture. It was interested in the way rooms train memory to stand in certain places. North would have been a distraction, a borrowed authority from maps that never had to explain why one chair mattered more than another.

The unanswered part is whether these rooms ever needed direction, or whether north was only borrowed confidence from maps with easier lives.
