The plan was to collect sounds too ordinary to survive: a gate dragging on concrete, a refrigerator inside a corner store, shoes crossing a wet lobby, the little click a bus shelter makes when wind moves through its loose panel.

Each pin had a listening instruction rather than a title. Stand under the pharmacy awning. Face away from traffic. Wait until the delivery door opens. Do not record the first sound; record the one that follows when the street returns to itself.

The map has no audio. Only pins where the recordings should have been, which may be the more honest archive.

## Sound Index

The map treats listening as a civic act, not a technical one. It suggests that places are partly made of sounds too small to become landmarks and too temporary to receive names.

What still bothers the record is this: whether the archive failed because nothing was captured, or succeeded because it left the city unpossessed.
