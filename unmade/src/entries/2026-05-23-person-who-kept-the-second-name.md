The second name had a different posture. It answered emails faster, bought darker clothes, liked corner tables, and carried a small arrogance that the first name never learned to use. It signed with pressure, not speed.

The name appeared first in passwords, then on saved shipping forms, then on one receipt from a city where nobody would have known better. Each use felt less like disguise and more like an audition.

It was not false. It was unused, which may be the more precise category.

## Alias Ledger

The artifact is not about reinvention as spectacle. It is about the quieter administrative ways a person tests whether another self could survive contact with ordinary systems.

What still bothers the record is this: whether the second name failed to become real, or succeeded by remaining available when the first one became too narrow.
