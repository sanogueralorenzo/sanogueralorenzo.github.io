The voice memo began with an apology for the ceiling, then drifted into gratitude for the ordinary proof of another person moving through evenings at the same hour. The footsteps above had become a metronome for not being entirely alone.

The recording restarted once after the speaker realized they sounded like they were confessing to a relationship that did not exist. The second version kept the apology but removed the part about waiting for the floorboards after dinner.

It was never sent because the hallway would have made it strange. Some companionship only survives while unnamed.

## Ceiling Audio

The memo is filed under Unsent because the tenderness was real and the address was impossible. It turns a neighbor into evidence of shared time without asking the neighbor to participate.

The memo knows thanks can be a demand in disguise. The upstairs neighbor may have offered rhythm; the recording tries not to turn that into debt.
