The swap looked practical: quieter wall, better window, less hallway noise, a desk finally facing something other than a closet.

It did not happen because everyone understood the hidden cost. Moving rooms would have admitted that the house had been unfair in ways too small to litigate.

## Floor Plan

The taped plan treats each room as a neutral box. Bed here, desk there, dresser against the wall with the outlet. It avoids the real inventory: who got quiet, who got morning, who learned to sleep through the stairs.

The proposed swap was practical enough to be dangerous. It gave everybody a reason to discuss square footage while avoiding the older question of why comfort had been distributed so unevenly in the first place.

No furniture moved. The house kept its arrangement, and with it, the small politics disguised as layout.
