The office had a counter, a bell, and forms for endings that had failed to become official: friendships dissolved by weather, projects abandoned without a final note, rooms left by degrees. Every form included a field for the person who knew first, and another for the person who refused to know.

The drawers were organized by ending type. Mutual, implied, postponed, inherited, denied. A separate cabinet was reserved for endings kept alive by one person alone.

Nobody staffed it. That was either the reason it failed or the most accurate thing about it.

## Waiting Number

The office is categorized as Unheld because it represents a life where closure becomes administrative. The appeal is obvious. So is the danger.

Its danger is administrative kindness. Give every unresolved thing a number and people may mistake waiting neatly for being released.
